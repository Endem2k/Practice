MAKOTO SHINKAI

LIFE & WORK

JUST COUNTRY BOY

Makoto Shinkai was born Makoto Niitsu on February 9, 1973, and his hometown is Nagano, Japan.

" When I was in elementary school, I wanted to become an astronaut.
I lived in a province where there was no entertainment, so I spent a lot of time looking at the sky and then it was reflected in my films. "

Later he moved to study in Tokyo, where he remained working as an employee of Minori. For 5 years of work there Shinkai showed himself as a very versatile personality: he participated in the creation of new video game exhibitions, was engaged in the layout of instruction booklets and support of the company's website.

FIRST STEP

Shinkai started out as a graphic designer for a video game company called Falcom. In 1999, Shinkai released his five-minute monochromatic animated short, "She and Her Cat."

Shinkai provided the voice for the cat, and his fiancee, Mika Shinohara, provided the voice for the cat's owner.

Some months later in June 2000, Shinkai was inspired to begin Voices of a Distant Star by drawing a picture of a girl in a cockpit grasping a cell phone. Some time later, he was contacted by Mangazoo, who offered to "work with him," giving him a grant to turn his idea into an anime they could sell. In May 2001, he quit his job at Falcom and began to work on Voices. In an interview, Shinkai noted that production took around seven months of "real work."

"I'm often asked why I don't make feature films,
but animation is something that allows you to do everything yourself, as you planned."

In 2002 he released The Voices of a Distant Star (Hoshi no Koe), which became his first short movie shown in theaters. Since both movies were made almost all by himself, their high quality surprised the viewers.

After, he released his first full movie - The Place Promised in Our Early Days.
Characters in the new tape compared to previous experiences significantly increased, there are side characters in the film. The team of developers was also replenished.
In addition to Shinkai himself, Illustrator Ushio Tazawa (character design) and Z-modeller Kazuno-ri Takada (Vellasela aircraft design) worked on the creation of the film.
But most of the work, true to his principle, the author took over.
The main thematic priorities in the new film remain unchanged:
falling in love, growing up, choosing the path and, of course, loneliness.

Shinkai's next project was 5 Centimeters per Second. The first part streamed on Yahoo! Japan to Yahoo! Premium members from 16 to 19 February 2007. On 3 March 2007, the full-length film had its theatrical premiere at Cinema Rise in Shibuya, Tokyo. The film consists of three segments: "Cherry Blossom" (Ōkashō), "Cosmonaut" (Kosumonauto), and "5 Centimeters per Second" (Byōsoku Go Senchimētoru)

Shinkai tried to reveal the basis, a kind of "universal law", universal for any life - and created a work where the main characters are time and distance. Here every frame, every segment of the plot is devoted to the four dimensions in their everyday, all-powerful habitual existence. A nontrivial poetic task: to pick up such metaphors and images that the viewer could pass through himself every second of life of heroes, every centimeter of the way passed by them - and keeping originality of history, to make it close and authentic.

The story is set in Japan, beginning in the 1990s up until the present day (2008), with each act centered on a boy named Takaki Tōno. The first act takes place during a time when cell phones are uncommon and email had not yet reached the general population.

Takaki tono quickly became friends with Akari Shinohara when she transferred to his elementary school. The two of them became close, thanks to common interests and Hobbies. As a result, they formed a close bond: they talk to each other using their given names without any form of deference, which is extremely unusual in Japan, even among people who are romantically inclined to each other. This fact is lost in the process of translation into English and other languages, which greatly reduces the implied closeness of their relationship.

After graduating from elementary school, Akari moves to Tochigi. The two keep in touch through correspondence, but despite the feelings they have for each other, they inevitably begin to distance themselves.

In the film, Shinkai demonstrates the fascinating city plans, which in everyday life do not pay due attention .

In 1999, Takaki is now in the third year of senior high in Tanegashima, where the Tanegashima Space Center is located. Kanae Sumida, a classmate of Takaki, has been in love with him ever since meeting him in junior high school but has never had the courage to confess her feelings. She tries to spend time with him, waiting long after school for the chance to travel home together. However, Takaki appears ignorant to Kanae's feelings and only treats her as a good friend. Kanae observes that Takaki is always writing emails to someone or staring off into the distance as if searching for something far away.

A distinctive feature of Shikai's work is getting awesome graphics: realistic urban landscapes and endless skies in which float the texture of the clouds and bright burning stars. At the same time, the artist presents accessible and penetrating stories against the background of the painted landscape.

" Nature is something very big, something with a very long history. If you look at the past, the life and activities of man did not play a significant role, they were almost invisible. But despite the vast scale of nature, people still managed to have an impact on it — and, unfortunately, not a good one. "

In the work of Makoto Shinkai, the train is an additional character. Material manifestation of the cosmic forces ruling in the human world. He can separate heroes or, on the contrary, connect them, become a silent interlocutor or a messenger of inexorable fate. Today, Tokyo and Nagano are connected by the Hokuriku Shinkansen high-speed rail line, which opened in 1997. The new line allows you to get from one city to another in just an hour and a half. However, in Sinkai's films we see the leisurely commuter trains of his student youth. Let not so fast and high-tech, but much more attractive to the romantic nature of the young animator.

In the third and final short, Takaki never finds spiritual peace, life seems meaningless to Him. He meets his young love, but only for a moment, after which the girl disappears again.

Trains are the only time when Makoto Shinkai resorts to the help of 3D graphics, which does not stand out much from the General background of the picture.
